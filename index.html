About Me

Avid Technologist and Master Coder in the Making

  • 2004-Present

    The BrainChild Design

    I enrolled in a Dreamweaver course and learned basic HTML and CSS and then started progressing into learning eCommerce. I wanted to provide a tool that could handle most, if not all, business operations. I made the transition to using WordPress and quickly evolved into becoming an pseudo-expert using Woocommerce. I learned how to design and build full business applications without using any code. My goal was to be able to deploy a website for a customer, in record time.

  • April 2009

    Transitioned to WordPress

    I started using WordPress as a design and development platform and quickly found that the utility far outweighed it's drawbacks and started recommending it to my clients. WooCommerce came of age as a eCommerce platform and I set my mind to become an expert in it's use. I deployed websites with shopping carts, downloadable products, memberships and physical products. I was able to help my clients monetize their business through eCommerce and intelligent SEO practicess

  • May 2010

    Created BrainChild Hosting

    I did some research and found a great company that provided excellent hosting and support. They allowed me to whitelist their services at a reasonable price so that I could provide my clients with a full-service website solution. This enabled me to have a familiar platform to develop applications and give reliable service to my clients.

  • March 2019

    UCLA Coding Bootcamp - Full Stack Developers

    I decided that I needed to increase my skill set. I always have had a passion to learn how to code and finally had the opportunity to attend UCLA Coding Bootcamp to become a Full Stack Developer specializing in the MERN Stack. This course has been one of the most fulfilling things that I have done in my life including graduating from UCLA as a Psychology major and obtaining my pilot's license. I believe Coding is my calling and I intend to master as many facets as I can with the goal of joining a team at a larger tech company.

  • August 2019

    Full Stack Developer

    I believe that I have the drive and experience to become a great contributor to a team of developers. Although I am still a junior Full Stack Developer, I learn fast and I am highly motivated to both learn and grow as a software engineer. This is my chosen path in life and business and I want to develop software both for business and to help improve the human condition.
